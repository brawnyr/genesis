# GOD — Vision & Identity

## What GOD Is

GOD is a playground for making music. Not a DAW. Not a workstation. Not a product. A personal instrument built from scratch because nothing else thinks the way you think.

You sit down, you play, you flow. When the flow is cold, you hit GOD and capture it. That's the whole idea.

## How It Works

You play freely — pads, knobs, whatever feels right — and when you're ready to build, you start the loop. The loop is a fixed-length cycle (1, 2, or 4 bars at a set BPM) and everything revolves around it. From there:

- **Layer** — every pad hit records into its own layer
- **Shape** — mute, unmute, transform with knobs, queue changes for the next loop boundary
- **Capture** — when the flow is right, hit GOD. It records the master output to a WAV file.

The arrangement isn't drawn on a timeline. It's performed. You queue up changes — bring in the snare, drop the hats, open the filter — and they all fire together on the boundary. The performance *is* the track.

## What GOD Is Not

- Not a DAW. No playlist, no piano roll, no timeline editing.
- Not a product. No marketplace, no plugins, no VSTs.
- Not trying to do everything. Every feature earns its place or it doesn't exist.
- Not a consumer tool. This is built by you, for you, from what you see in your mind.
- Not a packaged app. It runs as a raw binary from the terminal.

## The Vibe

The feel is the function. GOD should look and feel elite and sophisticated — not flashy, not generic. The terminal sets the standard: calm, intentional, monospace, information-dense. Every visual element matches that level. If something feels AI-crafted or generic, it's wrong. Sound, visuals, interaction — it's all one thing.
